Getting Better Results — Universal Tips

  1. Describe the style explicitly — Don't just describe the subject. Add: "cinematic photography", "watercolour illustration", "product photography on white background", "oil painting in the style of Rembrandt."
  2. Specify lighting — Lighting transforms images. "Golden hour", "studio lighting with soft box", "dramatic side lighting", "overcast natural light" all produce dramatically different results.
  3. Use aspect ratios intentionally — 16:9 for landscapes and hero images, 1:1 for social posts, 4:5 for Instagram, 9:16 for Stories and TikTok.
  4. Iterate, don't restart — Use the variation features to explore around a good result rather than starting from a new prompt. Small variations often surface the best version.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI image generator in 2026?
Midjourney v6 produces the highest-quality artistic images. For photorealism, DALL-E 3 and Adobe Firefly are strongest. For full control and no restrictions, Stable Diffusion run locally remains the most flexible option.
Which AI image generator is free?
Microsoft Designer (DALL-E powered) offers free daily credits. Adobe Firefly gives 25 free credits per month. Stable Diffusion is free if you run it locally on your own hardware.
Can I use AI-generated images commercially?
It depends on the tool. Midjourney allows commercial use on paid plans. Adobe Firefly is trained on licensed content and is explicitly commercially safe. Always check the specific tool's terms of service.
What is the difference between Midjourney and DALL-E?
Midjourney excels at artistic, stylised, and aesthetically striking images. DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT) is better at following precise text descriptions and producing accurate, literal interpretations of prompts. Different strengths for different use cases.
How do I get better results from AI image generators?
Be specific about style, lighting, mood, and composition. Reference specific art styles or photographers. Use negative prompts to exclude unwanted elements. Iterate — the first generation is rarely the best.
